Best for specific needs

Best for travel

The Osprey Daylite Plus shines as a do-it-all travel companion. Its lightweight frame and daisy-chain lash points let you attach extra gear, while the padded laptop sleeve keeps your devices secure. For a carry-on daypack that handles hikes, city tours, and flight essentials without breaking the bank, this is the one. Our pick: Osprey Daylite Plus Daypack.

Best for laptop commuting

If your daily carry includes a full-size laptop and you need quick airport-style access, the Thule Subterra 34L is a standout. The fold-open laptop compartment lets you leave your pack zipped during security checks, and the tuck-away straps keep it streamlined when you’re stowing it. It’s an investment, but one that pays off in polish and durability. Our pick: Thule Subterra 34L Laptop Backpack.

Best for everyday school

The Herschel Classic offers iconic style at a price that fits any student budget. Its single large compartment swallows notebooks and a lightweight laptop sleeve (sold separately), and the reinforced base holds up to cafeteria floors. For light loads and a timeless look, it’s a hard bargain to beat. Our pick: Herschel Classic Backpack.

Our verdict

For most people, the Osprey Daylite Plus offers the best all-around value, it’s comfortable, versatile, and genuinely affordable. If you can spend a little more, the Thule Subterra adds premium commuting features, while the Herschel Classic is a wallet-friendly style icon that’s perfect for light everyday use. Whichever you choose, you’re getting a reliable pack that won’t weigh down your wallet or your shoulders.
